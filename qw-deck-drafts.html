Build Deck First Drafts Faster

Generate slide-by-slide storylines with action headlines and visual suggestions.

The scenario: You need to build a 15-slide strategy deck for a PE-backed consumer brand. The engagement focused on geographic expansion. The blank page is the hardest part — use Copilot to generate a storyline you can build on.

Weak vs. Improved Prompt

Weak Prompt
"Help me make a PowerPoint about our findings."
You'll get generic slide titles like "Introduction," "Overview," "Conclusion" with no storyline, no action headlines, and no consulting structure.
Improved Prompt
"I'm building a 15-slide strategy deck for a PE-backed consumer brand. The engagement focused on geographic expansion. Draft a slide-by-slide storyline that includes: (1) executive summary, (2) market opportunity by region, (3) competitive landscape, (4) recommended markets for entry, (5) financial projections, (6) implementation roadmap. For each slide, give me: the headline (action-oriented, 1 sentence), 3–4 bullet points for the body, and a note on what visual/chart would work best."
You'll get a complete storyline with action titles, structured bullet points, and visual guidance — a real starting point for your deck.

Why the improved prompt works

  • Structure: Specifying the sections gives Copilot a logical flow to follow.
  • Action headlines: "Action-oriented, 1 sentence" prevents descriptive/boring titles.
  • Visual guidance: Asking for chart suggestions saves time when you move to design.
  • Client context: "PE-backed consumer brand" and "geographic expansion" shape the content and tone.

Try This on Your Next Project

I'm building a [N]-slide strategy deck for [CLIENT TYPE/CONTEXT]. The engagement focused on [TOPIC].
Draft a slide-by-slide storyline that includes:
1. Executive summary
2. [KEY SECTION 1]
3. [KEY SECTION 2]
4. Recommendations
5. Implementation roadmap
For each slide, provide:
- Headline (action-oriented, 1 sentence)
- 3-4 body bullets
- A note on the best visual or chart to use

Use the output as a starting point. The headlines are usually the most valuable part — they force a clear storyline.

Human Review Required

Before using deck outlines, verify that all facts are correct, the storyline reflects your actual analysis, and no AI-generated assumptions replaced your consulting judgment.

Quick Workflow

1

Define the Story Arc

Before prompting, write down: the audience (who), the goal (what decision or action), and 3-5 key points you need to make. This prevents generic slide-by-slide filler.

2

Generate the Storyline

Use the template prompt above. Review the output — check that Copilot's slide flow follows a logical "situation → analysis → so what → recommendation" structure.

3

Refine Headlines

Ask Copilot to improve specific slides: "Rewrite slide 3's headline as an action statement that conveys the key finding" or "Add a chart suggestion for the market sizing slide."

Practice

Rebuild a Recent Deck

Take a deck you created last month. Feed the key findings into Copilot using the template. Compare the AI-generated storyline to what you actually built. What's different?

Action Headlines Challenge

Take 5 descriptive slide titles from an existing deck (e.g., "Market Overview") and ask Copilot: "Rewrite these as action headlines that tell the reader the key takeaway."

Common Mistakes

✗ Using the AI storyline as your final deck structure
✓ Treat it as a first draft — rearrange, cut, and add your own insight

Copilot generates a reasonable structure but can't know your client's priorities or political dynamics. Always reshape the story.

✗ Asking for "a presentation about X" without specifying the audience
✓ Include audience role, decision to be made, and presentation length

A board presentation and a team working session need completely different structures, depth, and tone.
