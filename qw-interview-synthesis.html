Synthesize Interviews Faster

Turn a stack of interview notes into themes, tensions, and presentation-ready quotes.

The scenario: You've just wrapped up 8 stakeholder interviews for a strategy engagement. You have pages of notes and need to identify patterns, contradictions, and strong quotes for the client presentation. This usually takes half a day. With a well-structured prompt, you can get a solid first pass in minutes.

Weak vs. Improved Prompt

Weak Prompt
"Summarize these interview notes."
You'll get a flat summary that loses nuance. No themes, no contradictions, no usable quotes. You'll end up doing the real synthesis yourself anyway.
Improved Prompt
"I have notes from 8 stakeholder interviews for a strategy engagement. Synthesize them by: (1) identifying the top 5 recurring themes across interviewees, (2) noting where interviewees disagree or contradict each other, (3) flagging 3–5 quotes that would be strong in a final presentation, and (4) suggesting 2–3 hypotheses these themes support. Tag each theme with which interviewees mentioned it."
You'll get a structured synthesis with labeled themes, tension points, usable quotes, and hypotheses you can test — all traceable back to specific interviewees.

Why the improved prompt works

  • Structure: It asks for specific deliverables (themes, contradictions, quotes, hypotheses).
  • Traceability: "Tag each theme with which interviewees mentioned it" makes the output auditable.
  • Presentation-ready: Asking for quotes and hypotheses gives you content you can drop into slides.
  • Analytical depth: Asking for contradictions catches nuance that a simple summary misses.

Try This on Your Next Project

I have notes from [N] stakeholder interviews for a [TYPE] engagement. Synthesize them by:
1. Identifying the top 5 recurring themes across interviewees
2. Noting where interviewees disagree or contradict each other
3. Flagging 3-5 quotes that would be strong in a final presentation
4. Suggesting 2-3 hypotheses these themes support
Tag each theme with which interviewees mentioned it.

[PASTE YOUR INTERVIEW NOTES HERE]

For longer notes, use Copilot in Word. For shorter notes, Copilot chat works great. Always review and refine the synthesis with your own judgment.
