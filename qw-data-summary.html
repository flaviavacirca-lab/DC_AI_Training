Summarize + Interpret Data

Find the story in your data and draft "so what" statements for client presentations.

The scenario: You have a large dataset of survey results — 200 respondents answering questions about enterprise software purchasing decisions. You need to find the story in the numbers and create "so what" statements for a client presentation. Copilot can help you identify patterns and frame insights.

Weak vs. Improved Prompt

Weak Prompt
"What does this data say?"
You'll get a restating of the numbers — "45% said X, 30% said Y." Descriptive statistics with no interpretation, no segmentation, and no actionable insight.
Improved Prompt
"I have survey data from 200 respondents about their purchasing decisions in enterprise software. Help me: (1) identify the top 3 statistically notable patterns, (2) segment respondents by decision-making role (end user, IT buyer, executive sponsor), (3) highlight any counterintuitive findings, and (4) draft 2–3 'so what' statements I can use in a client presentation. Focus on actionable insights, not just descriptive statistics."
You'll get a segmented analysis with patterns, surprises, and presentation-ready "so what" statements that go beyond describing the data.

Why the improved prompt works

  • "So what" framing: Explicitly asking for actionable insight prevents Copilot from just restating numbers.
  • Segmentation: Breaking data by role reveals patterns you'd miss in aggregate.
  • Counterintuitive findings: These are often the most valuable insights for a client conversation.
  • Constraint: "Not just descriptive statistics" steers Copilot toward interpretation.

Try This on Your Next Project

I have [DATA TYPE] from [N] respondents/records about [TOPIC]. Help me:
1. Identify the top 3 statistically notable patterns
2. Segment respondents by [RELEVANT DIMENSION]
3. Highlight any counterintuitive findings
4. Draft 2-3 "so what" statements for a client presentation
Focus on actionable insights, not just descriptive statistics.

[PASTE DATA OR DESCRIBE KEY METRICS]

For large datasets, use Copilot in Excel. For summarized data, Copilot chat works well. Always validate statistical claims.

Human Review Required

Before using data insights, verify that numbers match the original data, statistical patterns are real, and "so what" statements are supported by evidence.

Quick Workflow

1

Prepare Your Data

Organize your data into a clear format — table, bullet points, or key metrics. Tell Copilot what the data represents, who collected it, and what question it's supposed to answer.

2

Generate the Interpretation

Paste data + the template into Copilot. Review the "so what" statements — do they connect data to business implications your client cares about?

3

Challenge the Findings

Ask: "What alternative explanations exist for these findings?" or "A skeptical board member would ask ___. How would we respond?" This stress-tests the interpretation.

Practice

Reinterpret Existing Data

Take survey results or data tables from a recent engagement. Run the template prompt and compare Copilot's "so what" statements to the ones in your deliverable.

Find the Counter-Narrative

After getting an interpretation, ask: "What would someone who disagrees with this conclusion argue? What data would they point to?" This builds more robust analysis.

Common Mistakes

✗ Pasting raw data without explaining what it represents
✓ Tell Copilot: what the data is, who provided it, and what question it answers

Without context, Copilot generates generic observations. With context, it generates "so what" insights relevant to your engagement.

✗ Treating correlation as causation in AI-generated insights
✓ Always ask "why" before accepting a causal claim from Copilot

Copilot may say "X drives Y" when the data only shows correlation. A client will push back on unsupported causal claims.
