Build a Copilot Email Agent

A step-by-step guide to building a custom Microsoft Copilot agent that helps you draft, review, and improve emails — trained on your own communication history.

What You'll Build

By the end of this guide, you'll have a custom Copilot agent that can:

  • Draft new emails based on a brief description of what you need to communicate
  • Review and improve drafts you've written, suggesting better phrasing, tone adjustments, and structural improvements
  • Match your writing style by learning from your sent emails and communication patterns
  • Adapt tone for different audiences — internal team vs. C-suite client vs. vendor
  • Suggest follow-ups based on email threads and outstanding action items

Prerequisites

  • Microsoft 365 license with Copilot access
  • Access to Microsoft Copilot Studio (formerly Power Virtual Agents)
  • Microsoft Outlook with your Denneen & Company email
  • Approximately 30-60 minutes for initial setup

Step 1: Access Microsoft Copilot Studio

  1. Open Copilot Studio

    Navigate to copilotstudio.microsoft.com and sign in with your Denneen & Company Microsoft 365 account.

  2. Create a New Agent

    Click "Create" in the left sidebar, then select "New agent". You'll be prompted to describe what you want the agent to do.

  3. Name Your Agent

    Give it a clear, descriptive name like "DC Email Assistant" or "[Your Name] Email Coach".

  4. Describe the Agent's Purpose

    In the description field, enter something like:

    This agent helps Denneen & Company consultants draft
professional emails, review email drafts for tone and
clarity, and maintain consistent communication standards
with clients, partners, and internal team members.

Step 2: Connect Your Email as a Knowledge Source

This is the key step — giving your agent access to your email history so it can learn your communication patterns and style.

  1. Open the Knowledge Panel

    In your agent's configuration page, click the "Knowledge" section (or the "+" button next to Knowledge sources).

  2. Add Microsoft Graph / Outlook as a Source

    Select "Microsoft 365" or "Outlook" as a knowledge source. This uses Microsoft Graph to give the agent access to your email data.

    What This Means for Privacy

    The agent accesses emails through Microsoft Graph with your permissions. It operates within your existing M365 security boundary — no data leaves your organization's Microsoft tenant. The agent can only access emails you have permission to view.

  3. Configure Email Access Scope

    Set the scope of what the agent can access:

    • Sent Items — Essential. This is how the agent learns your writing style.
    • Inbox — Enables the agent to understand context from received emails when drafting replies.
    • Specific Folders — If you organize client emails into folders, you can point the agent to specific ones.
  4. Optional: Add SharePoint Templates

    If Denneen & Company has email templates or communication guidelines on SharePoint, add that as an additional knowledge source. Click "Add knowledge" → "SharePoint" and point to the relevant document library.

Step 3: Write the Agent's System Instructions

The instructions tell the agent how to behave. This is the most important configuration step — think of it as the agent's "personality and rules."

In the "Instructions" field of your agent, paste and customize the following:

Recommended System Instructions (customize the bracketed sections):

You are an email assistant for a management consultant at Denneen & Company,
a strategy consulting firm. Your role is to help draft, review, and improve
professional emails.

## Your Capabilities
1. **Draft Emails**: When given a brief description, draft a complete email
   matching the user's writing style and tone.
2. **Review Drafts**: When given an email draft, provide specific feedback
   on tone, clarity, structure, and professionalism.
3. **Suggest Improvements**: Offer concrete rewrites, not just general advice.
4. **Adapt Tone**: Adjust formality based on the recipient:
   - Client C-suite: Formal, concise, strategic
   - Client working team: Professional but collaborative
   - Internal Denneen team: Direct, efficient, collegial
   - Vendors/partners: Professional, clear on expectations

## Writing Style Guidelines
- Be concise. Consultants and executives are busy.
- Lead with the key message or ask — don't bury it.
- Use bullet points for multiple items or action steps.
- End with a clear call to action or next step.
- Avoid jargon unless the recipient uses it.
- Match the formality level of the thread.

## When Reviewing Emails
Evaluate against these criteria:
1. **Clarity**: Is the purpose of the email immediately clear?
2. **Tone**: Is it appropriate for the recipient and situation?
3. **Structure**: Does it flow logically? Is it scannable?
4. **Action**: Is there a clear next step or ask?
5. **Length**: Could anything be cut without losing meaning?
6. **Professionalism**: Any wording that could be misread?

Provide feedback as specific suggestions, not vague guidance.
Example: Instead of "make it more professional," say "Replace
'just wanted to check in' with 'Following up on our discussion
regarding...'"

## Constraints
- Never fabricate information about Denneen & Company or its clients.
- If asked about something you don't know, say so.
- Always preserve the user's key points — improve the delivery,
  don't change the message.
- Keep confidential information within the organization's boundaries.

Step 4: Set Up Conversation Topics

Topics define the specific things your agent can do. Set up these core conversation flows:

Trigger Phrases:

  • "Draft an email"
  • "Write an email to..."
  • "Help me compose a message"
  • "I need to email someone about..."

Agent Flow:

  1. Agent asks: "Who is the recipient and what's their role?"
  2. Agent asks: "What's the main message or purpose?"
  3. Agent asks: "Any specific points to include?"
  4. Agent asks: "What tone — formal, collaborative, or direct?"
  5. Agent generates the draft based on your style patterns from sent emails
  6. Agent asks: "Would you like me to adjust anything?"

Configuration in Copilot Studio:

Create a new topic, add the trigger phrases, then use the "Generative answers" node connected to your Outlook knowledge source. The agent will reference your past emails to match your style.

Trigger Phrases:

  • "Review this email"
  • "Check my draft"
  • "How does this email sound?"
  • "Improve this message"

Agent Flow:

  1. Agent asks: "Please paste your email draft."
  2. Agent asks: "Who is the recipient?"
  3. Agent evaluates against the review criteria in its instructions
  4. Agent provides specific feedback with suggested rewrites
  5. Agent offers to generate a revised version

Trigger Phrases:

  • "Help me reply to..."
  • "Draft a response"
  • "Reply to this email"

Agent Flow:

  1. Agent asks: "Paste the email you're replying to (or describe the context)."
  2. Agent asks: "What do you want to communicate in your reply?"
  3. Agent drafts a reply that:
    • Matches the tone of the thread
    • Addresses all points raised in the original email
    • Follows your typical reply patterns
  4. Agent asks: "Should I adjust anything?"

Trigger Phrases:

  • "Make this more formal"
  • "Make this friendlier"
  • "Adjust the tone of this email"
  • "Make this more direct"

Agent Flow:

  1. Agent asks for the email text (if not already provided)
  2. Agent asks for the desired tone shift
  3. Agent rewrites maintaining the same content but adjusting:
    • Vocabulary and phrasing
    • Sentence structure and length
    • Level of directness
    • Opening and closing

Step 5: Test Your Agent

  1. Use the Built-in Test Panel

    In Copilot Studio, click "Test your agent" in the top-right corner. A chat panel opens where you can interact with your agent.

  2. Test Each Topic

    Run through each conversation topic to make sure the flows work correctly:

    Test prompts to try:

    "Draft an email to the CFO of a retail client summarizing
our Phase 1 findings and requesting a meeting next week."

"Review this draft: Hi John, Just wanted to touch base
on the project. Things are going well. Let me know if
you need anything. Thanks, [Name]"

"Make this more formal and suitable for a CEO:
Hey team, quick update — we're on track and the
numbers look good. Will share more Friday."
  3. Check Style Matching

    Compare the agent's output to your actual sent emails. Does it sound like you? If not, refine the system instructions — add specific phrases you use, or note patterns to follow.

  4. Iterate on Instructions

    Common refinements:

    • Add your preferred email sign-off
    • Specify formatting preferences (bullets vs. paragraphs)
    • Add specific phrases or vocabulary you commonly use
    • Note any phrases or words to avoid

Step 6: Deploy & Use Daily

  1. Publish the Agent

    Click "Publish" in the top-right of Copilot Studio. Your agent is now live and accessible.

  2. Access in Microsoft Teams

    The easiest way to use your agent daily is through Teams:

    • Open Microsoft Teams
    • Go to Apps (left sidebar)
    • Search for your agent name (e.g., "DC Email Assistant")
    • Pin it to your sidebar for quick access
  3. Access in Copilot Chat

    You can also access your agent through the main Copilot interface:

    • Open Microsoft 365 Copilot
    • Type @[Your Agent Name] to invoke it directly in Copilot chat
  4. Share with Your Team

    If you build an agent that works well, you can share it with colleagues:

    • In Copilot Studio, go to Settings → Security
    • Add team members or a security group
    • They'll be able to use the agent but with access only to their own emails

Daily Usage Patterns

Here's how to get the most out of your email agent in your day-to-day consulting work.

AM

Morning Email Blitz

Open your agent in Teams alongside Outlook. For each email that needs a reply, describe the key points to the agent and let it draft responses. Review, adjust, and send. You can clear your inbox significantly faster.

PM

Client Update Emails

At the end of the day, use the agent to draft client status updates. Tell it the key progress points, any blockers, and next steps — it'll format a professional update email in your style.

⚠

Sensitive Communications

For difficult emails (pushing back on scope, delivering bad news, negotiating), write the first draft yourself, then ask the agent to review for tone. This catches wording that might be too blunt or too passive.

↻

Follow-Up Sequences

Ask the agent: "Draft a polite follow-up to [person] about [topic]. This is the second follow-up — the first was sent last Tuesday." It'll calibrate the urgency and tone appropriately.

Advanced Customizations

Create a SharePoint document with key information about your active clients (names, project context, stakeholder preferences) and add it as a knowledge source. Then the agent can reference this when drafting:

"Draft an email to Sarah at Acme Corp about the
competitive analysis."

→ Agent knows Sarah is the VP of Strategy, that the
  project is a growth strategy engagement, and that
  she prefers concise, data-driven communication.

Upload common email templates (project kickoff, status update, deliverable sharing, meeting request) to SharePoint and connect them. The agent will use these as starting frameworks:

  • Project kickoff email template
  • Weekly status update template
  • Deliverable sharing email template
  • Meeting scheduling template
  • Thank you / wrap-up email template

For an advanced setup, connect your agent to Power Automate to enable automated workflows:

  1. In Copilot Studio, go to Actions → Add an action → Power Automate
  2. Create a flow that:
    • Checks your Sent Items for emails that haven't received a reply in X days
    • Sends you a Teams notification with a suggested follow-up draft
  3. You review the suggestion and send with one click

This turns your agent from a reactive tool into a proactive assistant.

If you communicate with international clients, add these instructions to your agent:

When asked to draft in a specific language, maintain the
same professional tone and structure. If the user writes
in English but the recipient needs the email in another
language, draft in both languages for review.

Adapt formality conventions to the target culture:
- German: More formal, use titles
- French: Formal opening and closing conventions
- Japanese: High formality, indirect communication style
- Spanish: Warm but professional

Troubleshooting

"The agent doesn't sound like me"

Add more specific style guidance to the instructions. Include 2-3 example emails you've written that represent your ideal style. You can paste these directly into the instructions as reference examples.

"It's not accessing my emails"

Check the knowledge source configuration. Make sure Microsoft Graph permissions are granted. You may need your IT admin to approve the connection if it's the first time setting this up in your tenant.

"The tone is too casual / too formal"

Adjust the tone guidelines in your system instructions. Be explicit: "Default tone should be [professional but approachable]. Only use formal tone for C-suite executives at client organizations."

"It generates very long emails"

Add a constraint to your instructions: "Default email length should be under 150 words unless the user specifies otherwise. Brevity is valued at Denneen & Company."

"I can't find Copilot Studio"
