Using Copilot Transcripts in Meetings

Get more from every meeting by turning on Copilot transcription and using it for follow-ups, action items, and synthesis.

When to Turn On Transcripts

Turn it on for:

  • Internal team syncs and working sessions
  • Client check-ins where you're the note-taker
  • Brainstorming sessions with lots of ideas flying
  • Steering committee meetings with complex decisions
  • Interviews where you have permission to record

Think twice for:

  • Sensitive HR or personnel conversations
  • Client meetings where recording hasn't been approved
  • Informal 1-on-1s where the other person may not expect it
  • External calls with NDAs or confidentiality concerns

Always announce it

When you turn on transcription, Teams shows a notification to all participants. But it's still good practice to say: "I'm turning on Copilot to capture notes — is that OK with everyone?" This builds trust and avoids surprises.

How to Enable It

1

Start or join a Teams meeting

Copilot transcription works in any Teams meeting — scheduled or ad hoc.

2

Click the Copilot icon in the meeting toolbar

It's in the top bar of the Teams meeting window. If you don't see it, click the "..." menu.

3

Copilot starts transcribing automatically

Once activated, Copilot listens and generates a real-time transcript. You can ask it questions during the meeting too.

What You Get

Real-Time Summaries

Ask Copilot "What have we discussed so far?" mid-meeting to check alignment without disrupting the flow.

Auto-Generated Recap

After the meeting, Copilot creates a summary with key topics, decisions, and action items — available in the meeting chat.

Searchable Transcript

The full transcript is saved and searchable. Find specific quotes or decisions without re-watching the recording.

Follow-Up Drafts

Use the transcript to draft follow-up emails, create action item lists, or feed into other Copilot prompts.

Post-Meeting Actions

Here's how to extract maximum value from your transcript after the meeting ends.

1

Review the recap in Teams chat

Copilot posts a summary automatically. Scan it for accuracy — fix anything it missed or misattributed.

2

Extract action items

Open the Copilot panel and ask: "List all action items with owners and deadlines." Copy into your project tracker.

3

Draft follow-up email

Use the transcript to draft a concise follow-up. See prompt examples below.

4

Feed into synthesis work

Copy relevant transcript sections into a Copilot chat for deeper analysis — theme identification, contradiction spotting, or quote extraction.

Prompt Examples

Use these prompts with Copilot in Teams after a transcribed meeting, or paste transcript text into Copilot chat.

Extract Action Items

Review the transcript from today's meeting and list all action items. For each, include: (1) the action, (2) who is responsible, (3) the deadline if mentioned. Format as a numbered list.

Draft Follow-Up Email

Based on this meeting transcript, draft a concise follow-up email to the attendees. Include: (1) key decisions made, (2) action items with owners, (3) next meeting date if discussed. Tone: professional and brief. Under 200 words.

Identify Key Themes

Analyze this meeting transcript and identify the top 3-5 themes discussed. For each theme: (1) summarize the main points, (2) note any disagreements or open questions, (3) flag any decisions that were made. Tag each theme with who raised it.

Catch Up on a Meeting You Missed

I missed this meeting. Give me a 2-minute summary: (1) what was the main topic, (2) what decisions were made, (3) are there any action items for me, (4) anything I need to follow up on?

Worked Example: Client Steering Committee

Here’s what a real post-meeting workflow looks like, from transcript to deliverable.

Meeting: 60-minute client steering committee with 8 attendees. Topics: Q2 progress review, Phase 2 scoping discussion, budget reallocation request.

Copilot was on for the full meeting. After the meeting, the transcript appeared in Teams chat (~12 pages).

Prompt used:

List all action items from this meeting. For each: the action, who is responsible, and the deadline if mentioned. Group by workstream.

Result: Copilot identified 9 action items across 3 workstreams. Two had specific deadlines mentioned. One was misattributed (Sarah said it, not Mark) — we corrected it manually.

Prompt used:

Draft a follow-up email to the steering committee. Include: decisions made, action items with owners, and the agreed next meeting date. Keep it under 200 words. Professional tone.

Result: The email was ready in ~30 seconds. We edited two sentences for accuracy, added a personal note at the top, and sent it 10 minutes after the meeting ended. Previously this would have taken 20–30 minutes.

Prompt used:

Analyze the discussion themes from this meeting. What were the top 3 concerns the client expressed? Were there any points of disagreement among the steering committee members? What signals should we pay attention to for Phase 2 scoping?

Result: Copilot surfaced a concern about timeline compression that we hadn’t explicitly noted during the meeting. It also flagged that two committee members had different views on budget allocation — useful context for the next conversation.

Total post-meeting time: ~10 minutes. Previous approach (manual notes + email + debrief): ~45 minutes.

Practice: Try It This Week

Exercise 1: Your Next Internal Meeting

Turn on Copilot transcription for your next internal team sync. After the meeting, use the “Extract Action Items” prompt above. Compare Copilot’s list to what you would have written manually.

Exercise 2: The 10-Minute Follow-Up

After a transcribed meeting, challenge yourself to send the follow-up email within 10 minutes using the “Draft Follow-Up Email” prompt. Track how long it actually takes vs. your usual approach.

Exercise 3: Theme Detection

After a longer meeting (45+ min), use the “Identify Key Themes” prompt. See if Copilot catches anything you missed while in the flow of conversation.
