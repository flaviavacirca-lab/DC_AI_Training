Market Research with Copilot

A practical guide for Denneen consultants on using Microsoft Copilot to accelerate every phase of market research — from scoping to synthesis to client deliverables.

AI-Assisted Research Workflow

Copilot doesn't replace your analytical judgment — it accelerates the mechanical parts so you can spend more time on insight and strategy. Here's how it fits into each phase of the research process.

1

Scope & Structure

Use Copilot to quickly generate research frameworks, define key questions, and identify information gaps before you begin.

Try this prompt:

Act as a strategy consultant. I'm starting a market sizing exercise
for the [industry] market in [geography].

Help me structure my research by providing:
1. A framework for sizing this market (top-down and bottom-up approaches)
2. The 10 most important data points I need to find
3. Recommended public data sources for each
4. Key assumptions I'll need to make and how to validate them

Format as a structured research plan I can share with my team.
2

Competitive Landscape Mapping

Quickly build competitor profiles, identify market segments, and map strategic positioning using structured prompts.

Try this prompt:

Act as a competitive intelligence analyst. Create a comprehensive
competitive landscape analysis for [market/industry].

For each of the top [N] competitors, provide:
| Competitor | HQ | Est. Revenue | Key Products/Services | Target Segment | Competitive Advantage | Recent Strategic Moves |

Then provide:
- A 2x2 positioning map suggestion (recommend the two best axes)
- 3 key takeaways about competitive dynamics
- Identified white space or underserved segments

Base this on publicly available information. Flag any data points
that are estimates vs. confirmed.
3

Industry Trend Synthesis

Process large volumes of industry information and distill them into the trends and forces that matter for your client.

Try this prompt:

I've gathered research on the [industry] market. Here are the key
data points and findings:

[Paste your notes, data points, or key excerpts]

Synthesize this into:
1. Top 5 macro trends shaping this industry over the next 3-5 years
2. For each trend: a) evidence supporting it, b) potential impact
   on [client type], c) strategic implications
3. Any contradictions or tensions in the data
4. 3 "so what" insights a CEO would care about

Present as an executive briefing. Use a confident, analytical tone.
4

Market Sizing

Build market size estimates faster by having Copilot help structure your calculations and identify assumptions.

Try this prompt:

Help me build a market size estimate for [specific market segment]
in [geography].

Approach:
- Use both top-down and bottom-up methodologies
- Show your work and all calculations
- Clearly label every assumption with a confidence level (high/medium/low)
- Cite public data sources where possible

Present as:
1. Top-down estimate with calculation chain
2. Bottom-up estimate with calculation chain
3. Triangulation: compare the two and provide a best estimate range
4. Sensitivity analysis: which assumptions, if wrong, would most
   change the answer?

Format the calculations as clear tables.
5

Deliverable Drafting

Transform your research and analysis into client-ready slide outlines, executive summaries, and narrative memos.

Try this prompt:

I need to create a [deliverable type] for [client context].

Here is the analysis and data I've developed:
[Paste key findings, data tables, insights]

Create a [N]-slide outline with:
- A clear storyline that builds from "situation" to "so what" to
  "recommended actions"
- For each slide: title (as an action statement), 3-4 bullets,
  and a suggested chart or visual
- An executive summary slide at the beginning
- A "next steps" slide at the end

The audience is [role]. The goal is to [desired outcome].
Use consulting-style action titles (e.g., "Premium segment
growing 3x faster than mass market").

Copilot in Excel for Research Data

When working with data in Excel, Copilot can help you analyze, visualize, and extract insights without writing formulas.

Analyze Data

"What are the trends in this data?
Highlight any outliers or anomalies.
Create a summary table showing
year-over-year growth rates."

Copilot will scan your spreadsheet, identify patterns, and create formula-driven summary tables.

Build Charts

"Create a chart comparing revenue
by competitor over the last 5 years.
Use a stacked bar chart with a
trendline for total market growth."

Describe the visualization you want and Copilot will build it from your data.

Clean & Transform

"Standardize the company names in
column A. Remove duplicates.
Convert revenue from strings like
'$1.2B' to numeric values in millions."

Let Copilot handle data cleaning tasks that would normally take manual effort.

Copilot in Word for Research Memos

Use Copilot directly in Word to draft, restructure, and refine research deliverables.

✏

Draft from Notes

Paste raw research notes into Word, then ask Copilot: "Transform these notes into a structured research memo with an executive summary, key findings, and methodology section."

🔄

Rewrite for Audience

Select a technical section and ask: "Rewrite this for a non-technical board audience. Focus on business implications, not methodology."

✂

Condense

Select a long section: "Condense this to 3 bullet points. Keep only the most strategically significant findings."

🔍

Identify Gaps

Ask Copilot to review your draft: "Review this research memo. What questions would a skeptical client ask? What data gaps should we address?"

Quality Control: When to Trust (and Not Trust) AI Research

Copilot Is Strong At

  • Structuring research frameworks and approaches
  • Synthesizing large volumes of text into key themes
  • Generating hypotheses and identifying patterns
  • Drafting narrative around your data and analysis
  • Reformatting data (tables, charts, summaries)
  • Brainstorming competitive positioning ideas
  • Creating first-draft slide outlines and storylines

Always Verify Independently

  • Specific revenue figures or market size numbers
  • Company-specific financial data or metrics
  • Recent events (last 6 months) or breaking news
  • Regulatory or legal details
  • Client-specific proprietary data
  • Exact competitive market share percentages
  • Causal claims ("X caused Y")

The Consultant's Rule

Use Copilot to go from 0 to 70% faster. Then apply your expertise to get from 70% to 100%. Never put AI-generated data in a client deliverable without verification. The AI accelerates your work — it doesn't replace your judgment.

Advanced Techniques

Instead of asking for a final answer, ask Copilot to show its reasoning step by step. This helps you spot logical gaps and build stronger analyses.

Walk me through how you would approach sizing the
[specific market] step by step.

For each step:
1. State what you're calculating
2. Show the formula or logic
3. State your assumption and confidence level
4. Show the calculation
5. Note what could make this estimate wrong

Don't just give me a final number — I want to see
and validate each step of the logic chain.

Ask Copilot to challenge your work before a client does.

Here is my market analysis and recommendations:
[Paste your analysis]

Now act as a skeptical board member who has been in this
industry for 25 years. Poke holes in this analysis:
1. Which assumptions are weakest?
2. What alternative explanations exist for the trends I've identified?
3. What data would you need to see before accepting these conclusions?
4. Where is my logic most vulnerable to challenge?

Be specific and rigorous.

When you have data from multiple sources, use Copilot to reconcile and triangulate.

I have market size estimates from multiple sources:
- Source A: [estimate and methodology]
- Source B: [estimate and methodology]
- Source C: [estimate and methodology]

Analyze the differences:
1. Why might these sources disagree? (different definitions,
   scope, methodology?)
2. Which estimate is most credible and why?
3. What's the best estimate range given all three sources?
4. How should I present this to a client who expects
   a single number?

Prepare for expert interviews and synthesize findings afterward.

Before the interview:

I'm interviewing a [role] at [company type] about [topic].
Generate 15 interview questions organized by theme:
- Market dynamics (5 questions)
- Competitive landscape (5 questions)
- Future outlook (5 questions)

Make them open-ended and designed to surface insights
we can't get from public data. Include 2-3 provocative
questions that test conventional wisdom.

After the interview:

Here are my notes from an expert interview with [role]:
[Paste notes]

Synthesize these into:
1. Top 5 key insights (with direct quotes where possible)
2. How these findings confirm or challenge our existing hypotheses
3. New questions or research threads this interview raises
4. Recommended next steps based on what we learned

Time-Saving Research Shortcuts

Quick SWOT

"Generate a SWOT analysis for [company] based on their public filings and recent news. Format as a 2x2 grid with 4-5 points per quadrant."

Porter's Five Forces

"Apply Porter's Five Forces to the [industry]. For each force, rate it High/Medium/Low and explain in 2-3 sentences. Identify the most critical force for a new entrant."

Executive Summary

"Write a 200-word executive summary of this research. Lead with the single most important finding. Use the 'situation-complication-resolution' structure."

Benchmark Table

"Create a benchmarking table comparing [companies] across these metrics: [list metrics]. Include a column for industry average. Highlight leaders and laggards."

Value Chain Analysis

"Map the value chain for [industry]. For each stage, identify key players, margin structure, and where disruption is happening or likely."

TAM/SAM/SOM

"Help me build a TAM/SAM/SOM analysis for [product/service] in [market]. Define each level, show the logic for narrowing, and present as a funnel diagram outline."
