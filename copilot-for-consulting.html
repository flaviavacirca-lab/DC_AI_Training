Workflow-based modules organized around how you actually work. Each module includes a scenario, prompt comparisons, and a template you can use immediately.

Research & Market Sizing

You're kicking off a new engagement and need to quickly build a fact base — market size, key players, industry dynamics. Instead of spending hours assembling data from multiple sources, use Copilot to generate a structured starting point.
Weak Prompt
"Tell me about the healthcare IT market."
Output: Generic Wikipedia-style overview with no structure or actionability.
Improved Prompt
"I'm a strategy consultant preparing for a client engagement in U.S. healthcare IT. Create a market brief that includes: (1) estimated TAM/SAM with data sources, (2) top 10 vendors by revenue, (3) key growth drivers for the next 3–5 years, (4) buyer segments by organization type. Format as a structured outline with headers."
Output: Organized brief with sections, specific companies, and a framework you can use in client prep.

Try This on Your Next Project

I'm a strategy consultant preparing for a client engagement in [INDUSTRY/MARKET]. Create a market brief that includes:
1. Estimated TAM/SAM with data sources
2. Top [N] vendors/players by revenue or market share
3. Key growth drivers for the next 3-5 years
4. Buyer segments by [RELEVANT SEGMENTATION]
Format as a structured outline with headers I can share with my team.

Replace the bracketed sections with your specific engagement details.

Interview & Qualitative Synthesis

You've completed 6–10 stakeholder interviews and need to identify patterns, contradictions, and strong quotes for your final presentation. Normally this takes half a day; Copilot can give you a solid first pass in minutes.
Weak Prompt
"Summarize these interview notes."
Output: Flat summary that loses the nuance and doesn't identify themes.
Improved Prompt
"I have notes from 8 stakeholder interviews for a strategy engagement. Synthesize them by: (1) identifying the top 5 recurring themes, (2) noting where interviewees disagree or contradict each other, (3) flagging 3–5 quotes that are strong for a client presentation, (4) suggesting 2–3 hypotheses these themes support. Tag each theme with which interviewees mentioned it."
Output: Structured synthesis with themes, tension points, usable quotes, and hypotheses.

Try This on Your Next Project

I have notes from [N] stakeholder interviews for a [TYPE] engagement. Synthesize them by:
1. Identifying the top 5 recurring themes across interviewees
2. Noting where interviewees disagree or contradict each other
3. Flagging 3-5 quotes suitable for a client presentation
4. Suggesting 2-3 hypotheses these themes support
Tag each theme with which interviewees mentioned it.

Deck Drafting & Storylining

You need to build a 12–20 slide strategy deck for a client presentation. The blank page is the hardest part. Use Copilot to generate a slide-by-slide storyline with headlines, bullet points, and visual suggestions.
Weak Prompt
"Help me make a PowerPoint about our findings."
Output: Generic slide titles with no storyline or consulting structure.
Improved Prompt
"I'm building a 15-slide strategy deck for a PE-backed consumer brand. The engagement focused on geographic expansion. Draft a slide-by-slide storyline: (1) executive summary, (2) market opportunity by region, (3) competitive landscape, (4) recommended markets, (5) financial projections, (6) implementation roadmap. For each slide: give the headline (action-oriented, 1 sentence), 3–4 body bullets, and a note on the best visual/chart."
Output: Full storyline with action titles, structured content, and visual guidance per slide.

Try This on Your Next Project

I'm building a [N]-slide strategy deck for [CLIENT TYPE/CONTEXT]. The engagement focused on [TOPIC].
Draft a slide-by-slide storyline that includes:
1. Executive summary
2. [KEY SECTION 1]
3. [KEY SECTION 2]
4. Recommendations
5. Implementation roadmap
For each slide, provide:
- Headline (action-oriented, 1 sentence)
- 3-4 body bullets
- A note on the best visual or chart to use

Market Mapping & Hypothesis Development

You need to build a competitive landscape for a client entering a new vertical. You want not just a list of competitors, but a structured map with positioning frameworks and testable hypotheses.
Weak Prompt
"List competitors in fintech."
Output: A flat list with no structure, segmentation, or strategic framing.
Improved Prompt
"I'm mapping the competitive landscape for a client entering B2B fintech payments. Create: (1) 15–20 key players categorized by segment (processing, infrastructure, embedded finance), (2) for each: estimated revenue range, funding stage, key differentiator, (3) a 2x2 framework for positioning (e.g., scale vs. specialization), (4) 3 hypotheses about where the market is headed. Format as a table."
Output: Structured competitive map with segmentation, a positioning framework, and forward-looking hypotheses.

Try This on Your Next Project

I'm mapping the competitive landscape for a client in [INDUSTRY/VERTICAL]. Create:
1. [N] key players categorized by segment ([SEGMENTS])
2. For each: estimated revenue range, funding stage, key differentiator
3. A 2x2 positioning framework (e.g., [AXIS 1] vs. [AXIS 2])
4. 3 hypotheses about where the market is headed
Format as a table I can paste into a deck.

Data Interpretation Support

You have survey data, financial metrics, or market data that needs to be turned into insights for a client presentation. You need the "so what" — not just descriptions of what the numbers say.
Weak Prompt
"What does this data show?"
Output: Restates the numbers without interpretation or actionable insight.
Improved Prompt
"I have survey data from 200 enterprise software buyers. Help me: (1) identify the top 3 statistically notable patterns, (2) segment respondents by role (end user, IT buyer, exec sponsor), (3) highlight counterintuitive findings, (4) draft 2–3 'so what' statements for a client presentation. Focus on actionable insights, not descriptive statistics."
Output: Pattern analysis, segmented view, surprises, and presentation-ready "so what" statements.

Try This on Your Next Project

I have [DATA TYPE] from [N] respondents/records about [TOPIC]. Help me:
1. Identify the top 3 statistically notable patterns
2. Segment by [RELEVANT DIMENSION]
3. Highlight any counterintuitive findings
4. Draft 2-3 "so what" statements for a client presentation
Focus on actionable insights, not descriptive statistics.

Internal Ops & Email Efficiency

Beyond client work, Copilot can speed up internal tasks — meeting prep, follow-up emails, status updates, and coordination across the team.
Weak Prompt
"Write an email about the meeting."
Output: Vague, overly generic email with no context or structure.
Improved Prompt
"Draft a follow-up email after our internal team sync on the [Client] engagement. Cover: (1) 3 decisions made today, (2) action items with owners and deadlines, (3) open questions for next week. Tone should be concise and collaborative. Under 200 words."
Output: Clean, structured follow-up ready to send with decisions, actions, and next steps.

Try This on Your Next Project

Draft a [EMAIL TYPE] for [AUDIENCE/CONTEXT]. Cover:
1. [KEY POINT 1]
2. [KEY POINT 2]
3. [KEY POINT 3]
Tone: [concise/formal/collaborative]. Under [N] words.
